## Minor Description

The Minor in Computer Science provides non-CS majors with foundational knowledge in computer science. Students must satisfy prerequisite coursework before enrolling in the core minor courses. After completing prerequisites, students complete three core CS courses and at least three additional SCS courses to fulfill the minor.

---

## Important Notes

- **Prerequisite courses (15-112 and 21-127) do NOT count toward the minor's unit total**
- Pre-requisites and two other courses used for the minor may double-count toward another major or minor
- General education courses do not count toward double counting allowances

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do prerequisites count toward the minor?
No. Courses 15-112 and 21-127 are prerequisites and do NOT count toward the minor's unit total.

### How many courses can double count?
Prerequisites can double count without limit. Additionally, up to 2 other courses from the core or elective requirements may double count with another major or minor (excluding general education courses).

### What course numbers are eligible for electives?
For both CS and CS/SCS electives, courses must be numbered 200 or above, with specific exclusions as listed in each section.

### Can I take more than the minimum electives?
Yes. The minor requires at least 1 CS elective and at least 1 CS/SCS elective, but students may take additional electives to deepen their knowledge.

### What is the difference between CS Elective and CS/SCS Elective?
- **CS Elective:** Must be from subject code 15 (Computer Science only)
- **CS/SCS Elective:** Can be from subject codes 02, 05, 10, 11, 15, 16, or 17 (broader SCS options)
