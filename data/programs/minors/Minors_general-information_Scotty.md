#### **Declaring a Minor at CMU-Q**

As a student at CMU-Q, you have the opportunity to pursue a **minor** (or multiple minors) in a variety of subject areas.

Students who wish to declare a minor are encouraged to do so **no later than the end of their sophomore year** to ensure they have enough time to complete all required courses before graduation.

#### **Key Information:**

-   Students with a **declared minor** may receive **priority enrollment** for courses required for the minor.
    
-   Minors may be **dropped or changed at any time** by emailing **Jarrin Nevel** [](mailto:jfn@andrew.cmu.edu)to ensure your degree audit is updated.
    
-   New application/declaration forms should be submitted **at least two weeks prior to registration week** in order to be considered for available spaces in non-major courses.
    
-   **Declaration does not guarantee enrollment** in courses, but may be used to determine priority—particularly for **graduating seniors**.
    
-   Students are responsible for tracking their own course requirements through the **Academic Audit**.

#### **Submission Guidelines:**

-   All forms are **PDF fillable**.
    
-   **Handwritten forms will not be accepted**.
    
-   Completed forms should be submitted via email to:  
    **qatareducationform@andrew.cmu.edu**

Arabic Studies

The minor in Arabic Studies promotes not just language proficiency but also an understanding of Arabic culture. Students must have an Advanced-level proficiency in the Arabic language. CMU-Q students who need elementary and intermediate level courses should consult with the campus advisor for Arabic Studies before declaring the minor. Student progress may be accelerated or supplemented by study abroad.

Students may double count one course taken for the Arabic Studies minor that are also being used to fulfill the requirements of other majors, minors, and programs. This does not include double-counting with General Education requirements, which has no limit.

Students with native or near-native proficiency in listening and speaking of the language prior to entering CMU should consult with the minor advisors for a different curriculum that may accelerate their completion of the requirement.

Biological Sciences

All university students are eligible to pursue a minor in biological sciences in conjunction with a major in any other department in the university. The minor serves to facilitate students who are interested in obtaining a broad-based perspective in biology. Students pursuing the minor in Biological Sciences will:

-   Acquire basic knowledge in the key areas of biological sciences, namely biochemistry, genetics and cell biology
-   Relate the foundational knowledge to more specialized areas through electives of their interest

A minimum of six biological sciences courses (and two chemistry prerequisites) must be completed to fulfill the minor in biological sciences. The curriculum includes four required courses and two elective courses as specified below. Units awarded for undergraduate research are not applicable to elective courses. Courses taken in other departments or colleges will be considered on an individual basis.

Computational Biology

Computational Biology is concerned with solving biological and biomedical problems using mathematical and computational methods. It is recognized as an essential element in modern biological and biomedical research. There have been fundamental changes in biology and medicine over the past two decades due to spectacular advances in high throughput data collection for genomics, proteomics and biomedical imaging. The resulting availability of unprecedented amounts of biological data demands the application of advanced computational tools to build integrated models of biological systems, and to use them to devise methods of prevent or treat disease. Computational Biologists inhabit and expand the interface of computation and biology, making them integral to the future of biology and medicine.

Computer Science

Students minoring in Computer Science, (CS) will obtain a basic level of understanding of the fundamentals of Computer Science. The CS the minor will also enable students to further explore sub-areas of interest within CS based on their interests. Students completing the minor will be able to use, design, develop, identify and analyze different algorithms and data structures to solve various problems. Students will also be able to reason about and implement these algorithms using different programming languages and paradigms that best suit the desired application domain.

Students interested in pursuing a minor in Computer Science should first consult with the CS minor advisor after completion of the prerequisites, 15-122, 15-150 and with at least one of the 200-level required courses in progress. Students are expected to complete all courses for the minor with a C or higher (for a minor average QPA of 2.0 or higher).

Financial Management

**Financial Management Minor**

This minor is designed for students who are interested in gaining an in-depth understanding of financial markets.  This minor mirrors the Finance concentration in the Business Administration program and would be beneficial to students who are interested in pursuing a career in the financial sector or management consulting.  Students will learn about core concepts in finance, such as time value, bond and equity valuations, project evaluation with the underlying goal of making more informed business decisions.  They will also gain exposure to financial modeling tools designed to help value companies in order to make investment decisions.

Students will have to take Principles of Microeconomics (73-102) as it is a prerequisite for the Finance course.  Interested students are encouraged to speak with the minor advisor to learn more about the choice of electives that would be better suited for their career goals.

Neuroscience

The curriculum within the Neuroscience minor will allow students from various disciplines to gain fundamental knowledge of neuroscience concepts. The interdisciplinary nature of the coursework echoes the nature of the field itself; students will select courses from the natural, social, and computer sciences.  Students pursuing the minor in Neuroscience will:

-   Acquire foundational knowledge of the basic biological foundations of the nervous system, from the cellular through systems levels.
-   Understand the effects of basic neurological function on behavior, including cognition.
-   Gain an appreciation of the interdisciplinary nature of the field of neuroscience.

Because the curriculum within this minor may overlap with some degree requirements, **no more than 2 courses** fulfilling Neuroscience Minor requirements **may count towards the requirements of a student’s major or other minor.**

Sociology

The Sociology minor introduces the student to central concepts in sociological theory and methods of empirical inquiry needed to broadly understand social behavior, including its structure, history, and dynamics. Students choose among a range of methodological approaches and substantive topic areas including social psychology, work and organizations, social networks, technology and society, medical sociology, and gender and family. Exposure to these topics will help students understand and appreciate the processes by which families, groups, and organizations form and evolve over time; by which individuals affect and are affected by the society in which they live; and by which technology and institutions shape and influence society. This background in empirical tools and social theory will strengthen the student’s ability to pursue graduate studies in sociology, social history, social science, and organizational theory; to begin professional careers involving social analysis, network analysis, data analysis of teams, groups and organizations, social analysis within journalism, political institutions, the government, and online; and to enter the corporate environment with a thorough understanding of organizational activity.

Tech Entrepreneurship

**Launch Your Startup with CMU-Q’s Tech Startup Minor**

Are you passionate about innovation and entrepreneurship? Carnegie Mellon University in Qatar (CMU-Q) offers the Tech Entrepreneurship Minor, designed for students from all majors who aspire to build their own tech-driven startups. Whether you come from Information Systems, Business, Computer Science, and Biology, this minor provides the skills, mindset, and network to turn your ideas into real-world ventures.

The program takes you through the full startup journey, from ideation and market validation to prototyping and pitching to investors. It emphasizes human-centric design and emerging technologies, ensuring that your startup creates local impact while being globally scalable—transforming ideas into viable, high-impact businesses.

One of the core strengths of this minor is multidisciplinary collaboration. Students form diverse startup teams, bringing together technical expertise, business strategy, and creative problem-solving. This mirrors real-world startups, where success depends on the synergy of people with different skills and perspectives.

By the end of the minor, you won’t just have a business plan—you’ll have a fully developed startup ready to scale. You’ll also gain mentorship, networking opportunities, and investor connections, giving you a competitive edge in Qatar’s growing startup ecosystem.
